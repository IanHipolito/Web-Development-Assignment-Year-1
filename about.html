Brief Incite of Paris Saint Germain

Paris Saint-Germain Football Club , commonly referred to as Paris Saint-Germain, PSG, Paris or Paris SG, is a professional football club based in Paris, France. They compete in Ligue 1, the top division of French football. Paris Saint Germain is one of France's most successful club to date, they have won over 40 official honours, including nine league titles and one major European trophy. Their home ground is the Parc des Princes which is a beautiful stadium filled with amazing fans every single time they play. Paris Saint Germain is currently owned by Qatari businessman Nasser Al-Khelaifi wgo is the head of Qatar Sports Investments, which owns and operates French team Paris Saint-Germain.

History

In the summer of 1970, an ambitious group of businessmen decided to create a major team in the French capital. Guy Crescent and Pierre-Étienne Guyot chose to merge their newly formed side, Paris Football Club, with Stade Saint-Germain of Henri Patrelle after the team from Saint-Germain-en-Laye, 15km west of Paris, were promoted to Ligue 2. The three men were stuck with the financial feasibility of the project, however they met with Real Madrid's president Santiago Bernabéu.

Bernabéu told them that starting a crowdfunding campaign was the best solution to establish a new team. After a petition was signed by 20,000 people, Paris Saint-Germain Football Club were officially formed on 12 August 1970.

Following a delicate financial situation, the Paris City Council offered to pay the club's debt and in return demanded them to adopt the more Parisian name of "Paris Football Club". After PSG president Henri Patrelle refused the name change and financial support was withdrawn, the club split in 1972. Paris FC remained in the top tier, while PSG were administratively relegated to Division 3.

After many ups and downs throughout the years with finances and mismanagement, Paris Saint Germain managed to climb up the ranks and divisions and finally made it back into the first divison of Ligue 1 in 1974 and remained there since.

Present

Paris Saint Germain changed dramatically when Qatar Sports Investments purchased the club in 2011. This not only made Paris Saint Germain the richest club in France but one of the wealthiest clubs in the world. Club president Nasser Al-Khelaifi pledged to form a team capable of winning the UEFA Champions League and making the club France's biggest name. However they have yet to claim the UEFA Champions League title.

From then on the club began to make some of the biggest signings in football history to date. This included Zlatan Ibrahimović, Thiago Silva, Edinson Cavani, David Luiz and many more, who were coached and managed by Carlo Ancelotti,. These signings led the team to great success, winning them a number of trophies throughout the years. Despite the departure of Carlo Ancelotti, PSG kept its winning ways under Laurent Blanc.

After losing 6-1 to Barcalona in the second leg of the round of sixteen, despite winning the first leg in France by a score of 4-0. PSG signed Neymar Jr. for a world-record fee of €222 million, making him the most expensive player ever and French prodigy Kylian Mbappé on an initial loan, which was made permanent in 2018 in a transfer worth €180 million plus add ons, making him both the second-most expensive player and most expensive teenager. Paris Saint Germain continues to make massive transfers such as signing Achraf Hakimi, Georginio Wijnaldum, Sergio Ramos, Gianluigi Donnarumma and Lionel Messi in just one year (2021). Although having a number if different managers such as Unai Emery, Thomas Tuchel and Mauricio Pochettino they have not yet won a Champions League Trophy.
